Hello, I am the
Rock

I was a promising football prospect and received offers from many Division I collegiate programs. I decided on a full scholarship from the University of Miami where I mostly played defensive tackle. In 1991, I was on the Miami Hurricanes' national championship team. I would appear in a backup role, starting only one game in four years, playing behind players such as Pro Football Hall of Famer Warren Sapp. After I graduated in 1995 with a Bachelor of General Studies in criminology and physiology, I signed with the Calgary Stampeders of the Canadian Football League as a linebacker. I was assigned to the practice roster but was cut two months into the season.
